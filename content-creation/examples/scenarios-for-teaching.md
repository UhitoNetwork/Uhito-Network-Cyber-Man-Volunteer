# Scenarios for Teaching – Practice with Your Community

Use these short scenarios to help people understand real situations.

---

## Scenario 1: The Urgent Email
**Situation:** Ahmed receives an email that looks like it's from his bank. It says his account will be closed unless he clicks a link and confirms his details.

**Question:** What should Ahmed do?

**Answer:** Don't click. Go directly to the bank's website (type the address) or call the number on his card.

---

## Scenario 2: The Friend in Trouble
**Situation:** Fatima gets a message on WhatsApp from her friend's number. The friend says they're in trouble and need gift cards urgently.

**Question:** What should Fatima do?

**Answer:** Call the friend directly using her real phone number. The friend's account may be hacked.

---

## Scenario 3: The Free Charging Station
**Situation:** Karim is at the airport, phone almost dead. He sees a free USB charging station.

**Question:** Is it safe to use?

**Answer:** Better to use his own charger and a wall outlet. Public USB ports can install malware. Use a data blocker if he must.

---

## Scenario 4: The "Microsoft" Call
**Situation:** Layla gets a call from someone saying they're from Microsoft. They say her computer has a virus and ask for remote access to fix it.

**Question:** What should Layla do?

**Answer:** Hang up. Microsoft never calls about computer problems.

---

## Scenario 5: Strange SMS
**Situation:** Hassan gets a text saying his package couldn't be delivered and to click a link to reschedule.

**Question:** What should Hassan do?

**Answer:** Check the tracking on the official delivery website – don't click the link in the message.

---

**Use these in workshops or one‑on‑one conversations.**
